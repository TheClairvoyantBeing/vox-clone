 The Walls Were Breathing (Originally Posted to r/nosleep 3 Days Ago - OP Deleted After)

OP here. Not sure if this belongs here, but I need to get this out before I lose my mind. Throwaway account because honestly, if my landlord finds out I’m posting this… well, let’s just say I’d rather face whatever’s in the walls than him. Moving on. 

I took this studio apartment in a converted factory building downtown last month. Cheap as dirt – $600 utilities included. Yeah, I knew there was a catch. The place smelled faintly of mildew and old oil, and the radiator clanked like a dying animal, but beggars can’t be choosers, right? I’m a freelance graphic designer, work mostly from home, needed somewhere quiet and affordable. Seemed perfect. 

For the first two weeks, it was fine. Quiet. A little too quiet, maybe – you could hear your own heartbeat in the dead hours between 3 and 4 AM. But then… the breathing started. 

Not literal breathing, at first. More like… a pulse. A slow, deep throb coming from the plaster behind my bed. I’d lie there, staring at the ceiling, and feel it through the mattress. Thoom… thoom… thoom… Like a heartbeat, but too slow. Too big. I blamed the building settling, the old pipes. Tried white noise. Didn’t help. The rhythm was too deliberate. Too… alive. 

Then came the warmth. 

It started subtly. Just a spot on the wall above my pillow that felt slightly warmer than the rest, like someone had pressed their palm there and held it for a minute. I touched it one night, half-asleep, fingertips brushing the paint. It wasn’t just warm – it was humid. Like breathing on cold glass. I jerked my hand back. Heart pounding. Checked for leaks, condensation – nothing. The wall was dry. But the warmth lingered where my fingers had been. 

I started sleeping with the light on. Stupid, I know. But that wall… it felt watchful. 

Last Tuesday, I came home from a client meeting exhausted. Dropped my bag, kicked off my shoes, and headed straight for the bed. I didn’t even turn on the light – just collapsed, face first into the pillow. And that’s when I heard it. 

Not throbbing. Not pulsing. 

Breathing. 

A slow, wet inhalation right behind my ear. Like someone had pressed their face into the wall and was sucking air through the plaster. Huuuuuuuuh… Then a pause. Then a slow, shuddering exhalation that made the hairs on my arms stand up: Haaaaaaaaah… 

I froze. Didn’t dare move. Didn’t dare breathe. The sound came again – closer this time. Huuuuuuuuh… (inhale) … Haaaaaaaaah… (exhale). It wasn’t in the wall anymore. It was in the room. With me. 

I squeezed my eyes shut, praying it was sleep deprivation, stress, something. I counted to ten. Twenty. When I opened my eyes, the room was dim with streetlight filtering through the blinds. Empty. Just me and the hum of the fridge downstairs. I sat there shaking for twenty minutes. Then I turned on every light in the place. Tore the apartment apart. Checked behind the fridge, under the sink, inside the closet. Found nothing but dust bunnies and a weird, sticky residue on the baseboard near the bed – like dried syrup, but clear and odorless. I wiped it off with a paper towel. It left no stain. Last night… it got worse. 

I decided to fight fire with fire. Or rather, light with light. I strung up those cheap LED strip lights all around the bedroom – behind the TV, under the desk, lining the ceiling. Bathed the room in harsh, blue-white light. Felt safer. Almost fell asleep. 

Then, at 3:17 AM, I woke up to the sound of peeling. 

Not loud. Not dramatic. Just a soft, persistent shhhhk… shhhhk… shhhhk… like someone slowly pulling tape off a surface. I didn’t open my eyes. I knew what it was. I lay there, rigid, listening. 

Shhhhk. 
Shhhhk. 
Shhhhk. 

It was coming from all the walls. Simultaneously. Like the room itself was unzipping. 

I finally cracked my eyes open – just a slit – and saw it. 

Where the LED strip met the wall near the ceiling, the paint wasn’t just peeling. It was retracting. Like a slow-motion tide pulling back from shore. Beneath the lifted edge of paint wasn’t drywall or insulation. It was… skin. 

Pale, slightly translucent skin, stretched tight over something lumpy and moving underneath. Veins like thin blue threads pulsed faintly beneath the surface. And it was breathing. The rise and fall was slow, deep, and utterly inhuman – each inhale taking nearly five seconds, each exhale a shuddering groan that vibrated through the floorboards. 

I saw it on three walls before I squeezed my eyes shut again. The ceiling too, I think. The breathing wasn’t just in the walls anymore – it was the walls. 

I didn’t sleep. I just lay there until dawn, listening to the soft, wet shhhhk of skin separating from whatever was underneath, and the incessant, alien thoom… thoom… thoom… of whatever heart was beating in the apartment’s bones. 

This morning, I peeled back a corner of the LED strip near the door. Just a tiny bit. The skin underneath was warm. And when I touched it… it flinched. 

I’ve packed a bag. Got my laptop, my phone, my ID. I’m leaving in ten minutes. I’m going to stay with a friend in the suburbs. 

But I’m scared. 

Not just of whatever’s in the walls. 

Because as I was packing, I noticed something. 

The sticky residue on the baseboard? It’s back. A fresh, glistening patch, right where I wiped it off last night. 

And it’s warm.
